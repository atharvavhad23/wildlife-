1. Executive Summary

The Wildlife Intelligence Platform utilizes a hybrid, tri-model architecture designed to solve three distinct conservation challenges:

  • Density Regression: Predicting the exact population count per km².
  • Occurrence Classification: Translating chaotic population shifts into actionable risk trends (Rising, Stable, Declining).
  • Spatial Clustering: Grouping massive geographic datasets into sub-second interactive heatmaps.

The pipeline is split across four distinct taxonomic domains: Animals, Birds, Insects, and Plants. Each domain leverages an algorithm specifically tuned to its unique biological behavior.

2. Animals (Mammals & Reptiles)

Because animals are highly mobile and participate in complex predator-prey dynamics, the models are trained to aggressively filter out environmental noise.

A. Density Forecasting Engine

  • Algorithm Selected: GradientBoostingRegressor
  • Hyperparameters: n_estimators=300, max_depth=7, learning_rate=0.05
  • Feature Engineering: Uses Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to extract the Top 8 variance-weighted features (e.g., rainfall, human disturbance).
  • Performance Metrics:
    • R² Accuracy: ~89.2%
    • RMSE: 0.124

3. Birds (Avian Species)

Avian species follow predictable, hard-coded migratory paths. Therefore, the models are heavily weighted toward temporal features (seasons, months) and spatial coordinates.

  • Hyperparameters: n_estimators=250, max_depth=8
  • Feature Engineering: Emphasizes season_enc, month, and lat/lon_grid intersections.

4. Insects (Invertebrates)

Insects are highly vulnerable to micro-climate shifts. A single temperature drop can decimate a population. The algorithms here focus on climate-interaction mathematics.

A. Density Forecasting Engine

  • Algorithm Selected: RandomForestRegressor
  • Hyperparameters: n_estimators=400, min_samples_split=4
  • Feature Engineering: Combines temperature and humidity to form highly sensitive micro-climate indicators.
  • Performance Metrics:

5. Plants (Flora & Vegetation)

Unlike animals or birds, plants are geographically locked. The models discard short-term weather anomalies and focus purely on static geographic grids and long-term soil/ecosystem health.

A. Density Forecasting Engine

  • Algorithm Selected: LinearRegression / Ridge
  • Hyperparameters: alpha=1.0 (L2 Regularization)
  • Feature Engineering: Because plants are stationary, the model is highly sensitive to multicollinearity. We implemented continuous "mean-padding" to prevent the exponential explosion of coefficients.
  • Performance Metrics:
    • R² Accuracy: ~94.1% (Highest accuracy due to stationary nature)
    • RMSE: 0.062
